The Reality in Chicago

The Animals Coming Through Our Door Can't Wait.

Chicago's shelters are under real pressure. Adoption rates have slowed. Stray numbers have climbed. And euthanasia at city shelters is up 25%. Anti-Cruelty is one of the last lines of defense — and we turn no animal away.

🐾

Animals Arrive Frightened and Broken

Emaciated dogs. Unsocialized cats. Pets surrendered because their owner lost their housing. Every animal gets a fresh start here — but only because donors make that possible.

Chicago's Shelter System Is at Its Limit

City shelters are overcrowded. Euthanasia rates have climbed 25% in recent years. Anti-Cruelty steps in as a safety net — taking animals that would otherwise have no place to go.

Most Organizations Turn Animals Away

Why Anti-Cruelty

Chicago's Oldest. Most Comprehensive. Always Open.

Anti-Cruelty doesn't just shelter animals. We are the full safety net — from emergency care and behavior rehab to free community clinics and cruelty investigations. We've been doing this since 1899, and we've never turned an animal away.

  • 4-Star Charity Navigator rating for financial health and transparency
  • Platinum GuideStar rating — the highest standard for nonprofit accountability
  • One of the highest-volume spay/neuter clinics in the entire country
  • Free behavior hotline, community wellness fairs, and pet food pantries for underserved Chicagoans
  • SAFE program: temporary housing for pets of people in crisis
  • Over 650 volunteers giving 50,000+ hours every year

Everything You Wanted to Know

Every dollar supports Anti-Cruelty's core programs: sheltering, veterinary care, spay/neuter services, behavior rehabilitation, community clinics, and education outreach. Anti-Cruelty holds a 4-Star Charity Navigator rating and Platinum GuideStar transparency rating, meaning your gift is managed with the highest accountability standards.

The Open Paws Circle is Anti-Cruelty's monthly giving community. Depending on your giving level, you receive monthly impact stories, photos of animals your gift helped, access to exclusive shelter events and presentations, and recognition in our annual report. It is our way of making sure you feel connected to the lives your generosity changes.

No. Anti-Cruelty is a fully independent private nonprofit and does not receive government assistance. This means every dollar in our budget comes from individual donors, foundations, and community support. Your monthly gift directly sustains everything we do.

Anti-Cruelty is a fully open-admission, unlimited-stay shelter. We accept any animal, for any reason, with no limitations on how long they stay. Dogs, cats, and other animals that might be turned away elsewhere will always find an open door here.

Anti-Cruelty was founded on January 19, 1899, by a group of Chicago women — led by Rose Fay Thomas — who believed the city's animals deserved protection and care. In 2024, we celebrated 125 years of continuous service to Chicago's animals and communities.

Absolutely. Anti-Cruelty has over 650 active volunteers who contribute more than 50,000 hours of care each year. Whether you want to walk dogs, socialize cats, support events, or foster an animal in your home, there is a role for you. Visit anticruelty.org/volunteer to learn more.

SAFE stands for Short-term Accommodations For Emergencies. It provides temporary shelter for pets whose owners are facing a personal crisis such as hospitalization, domestic violence, or housing loss. A monthly gift helps fund this critical safety net for both people and their pets.
